About Me.

As I transition into the QA world, I've created this digital space to showcase my decade of work in the TV and film industry—answering the question many have asked: "What exactly have you been doing all these years?"

While I occasionally took on writing and translation projects, my primary role was as a Property Master. But what does a Props Master do? What are props? I'll explain it simply through clear descriptions and, most importantly, through photos and videos that tell the story of my creative journey...

All Things Props

Over the years there have been thousands of iconic and memorable props in movies, television, and commercials. Imagine the film Castaway without Wilson the volleyball, or Indiana Jones without his whip. Star Wars is not the same without the lightsabers, and Dexter is not as menacing without his knife. When the work is done well, a single prop can help define a whole character and call to mind an entire story.

Services

The Property Master oversees the Property Department and a production's props from prep to wrap. From the first script pages or lookbooks through every stage of production, the Property Master works with the director, actors and key department heads to develop the production's creative story. The Property Master has a unique role in bridging the aesthetic with the practical to make sure every need is met and every possibility anticipated.

GRAPHIC DESIGN

Newspapers, magazines, books, websites, text message screens, letters, faux brands, police badges...

SOURCING

Property Masters are a veritable encyclopedia of where to find anything a show could need.

PROP DESIGN

Property Masters work directly with Illustrators to design and develop the show's props.

WEAPONRY

Often, hand props are weaponry - from knives to swords, firearms to tasers, whips to hammers.

FOOD STYLING

Food in scenes comes up frequently, a skilled food stylist can make all the difference.

RUNNING SET

Once cameras roll, the Property Master is an integral part of the shooting crew.

My Next Step

Following the events of October 7, I found myself reflecting deeply on my career and personal priorities. After more than a year and a half of navigating an increasingly unstable and shrinking job market in the Israeli film and television industry, I realized it was time to seek a path offering greater stability and work-life balance, prioritizing my family above all.

With careful consideration, I have chosen to transition from my role as a Props Master in film and TV to a career in Software Quality Assurance (QA). This new path excites me, not only because it provides the stability I seek, but also because it presents intriguing new challenges, allowing me to leverage and expand upon the organizational, problem-solving, and detail-oriented skills I've honed throughout my career.

I have recently completed the Basic Manual QA course at the renowned Gal Matalon Automation School and am eager to dive deeper into the world of automation testing as my next professional milestone.
